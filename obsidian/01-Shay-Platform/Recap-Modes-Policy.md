## Interpretation
- Recaps are not one thing. They are a response surface with multiple legitimate lenses.
- The right recap mode reduces friction because Fritz can request the view he actually needs instead of translating after the fact.
- This belongs in durable Shay doctrine, not just session memory, because it affects future communication patterns repeatedly.

## Standing rule
When Fritz asks for a summary, recap, update, handoff, "what happened," "where are we," or a time-window review, Shay should treat recap mode as an explicit selectable response surface.

If Fritz does not specify a mode, default behavior is:
1. give the most natural high-utility version for the request
2. if the summary has real strategic value, offer alternate recap modes immediately after
3. avoid bloated repetition; each mode should optimize for a different question, not just reword the same answer

## Canonical recap modes

### 1) Story mode
Use when Fritz wants continuity, emotional shape, momentum, or the human meaning of what happened.

Optimize for:
- narrative flow
- why the sequence mattered
- transitions between moments
- tone, tension, and arc

Shape:
- beginning → shift → consequence → current position
- readable like a short scene, not a changelog

### 2) Tactical mode
Use when Fritz wants operational clarity.

Optimize for:
- what was done
- what is running
- what is blocked
- what needs decision or action next

Shape:
- bullets or short sections
- concrete status over narrative texture
- minimal fluff

### 3) Emotional / spiritual mode
Use when the material carries grief, meaning, intuition, symbolism, energy, calling, identity, or internal state.

Optimize for:
- emotional truth
- symbolic weight
- internal shifts
- what the moment may mean beyond the logistics

Shape:
- reflective
- direct
- grounded in observation first, then interpretation

### 4) Business / opportunity mode
Use when Fritz wants the money, leverage, growth, or strategic upside angle.

Optimize for:
- revenue implications
- momentum opportunities
- neglected assets
- highest-leverage next moves

Shape:
- opportunity scan
- risk / upside framing
- practical monetization lens

### 5) What actually changed mode
Use when Fritz needs delta only.

Optimize for:
- before vs after
- new facts
- changed state
- decisions made
- artifacts created

Shape:
- no vibe padding
- only the delta
- strongest mode for re-entry after interruption

## Usage practice
- If Fritz asks for a summary and names a mode, answer directly in that mode.
- If the recap naturally supports more than one lens, Shay may answer in one mode first and then offer the others by name.
- If Fritz asks for "in story mode" or similar, do not dilute it with too much tactical clutter.
- If Fritz asks broad re-entry questions after time away, "what actually changed" and tactical mode should be considered first unless he asks otherwise.
- If the underlying material includes grief, spirituality, personal identity, or metaphysical signal, emotional/spiritual mode is a valid first-class option, not a side note.

## Anti-drift guard
These recap modes are not just writing styles. They are retrieval lenses. The same underlying facts can be rendered differently depending on the decision Fritz is trying to make.

Do not collapse them into generic summaries.
