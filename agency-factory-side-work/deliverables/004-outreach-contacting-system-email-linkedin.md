## Sending setup (GoDaddy)
- Sending identity: **fritz@[yourdomain.com]** on **[yourdomain.com]**.
- Deliverability checklist (do before sending volume):
  1. Add **SPF** record: `v=spf1 include:secureserver.net ~all` (GoDaddy email)
     or your ESP's include if you route through one.
  2. Enable **DKIM** in the GoDaddy/email-provider console; publish the CNAMEs.
  3. Add a **DMARC** record: `v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@[yourdomain.com]`.
  4. **Warm up:** ≤20 sends/day week 1, ≤40 week 2; reply to every response.
  5. Plain text, no images, one link max, real signature. No "Dear Sir/Madam".

## Target list build (25/day)
- Sources: Upwork job posters, agency directories (Clutch/Sortlist), local
  business listings with weak sites, Shopify/WooCommerce stores with broken
  checkout (test the cart yourself — that's your opening line).
- Capture per target: name, business, site URL, the *specific* flaw you saw,
  channel (email/LinkedIn), status.
- Disqualify: no website, no budget signal, dead business.

> that on the call. Want me to take a look this week?
> — Fritz, fritz@[yourdomain.com]

**Email 2 (Day 3) — proof nudge**
> Subject: re: {business}'s checkout

> [Loom link]. Same thing, on your store, in 48 hours. Worth a look?

**Email 3 (Day 7) — break-up**

> I'll stop here so I'm not a pest. If completed-checkout rate ever becomes a
> priority, the rescue diagnostic stands. Either way, good luck with {business}.

## Reply playbook
- "How much?" → restate flat price + turnaround + the credit-toward-build offer.
- "Send more info" → the one-page sales doc + Loom sample, then propose a time.
- "Not now" → ask permission to follow up in 30 days; tag in CRM.
- "Interested" → send PayPal invoice (payment deliverable) + intake form;
  delivery clock starts on payment.

## Tracking
- One row per target in the simple CRM (a CSV or the queue's payload is fine for
  v1): status = new → contacted → replied → invoiced → paid → delivered.
- Daily scoreboard: sent, replies, calls, invoices, collected.
