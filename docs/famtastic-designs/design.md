# FAMtastic Designs — Design System
_Created: 2026-06-11 · Child doc of mythos-foundation-plan.md (§34 item 6)_

This is the build-ready design spec for the Foundation MVP showroom (Next.js App Router + React Three Fiber + drei + GSAP ScrollTrigger + Tailwind — plan §28 Option A, launch discipline Option B). It feeds the Immersive/3D Design Planner's `ScenePlan` inputs (agents.md §1.4) and the UX/Site Architect's `PageSpec` rules. Every value below is a default a builder applies without asking. Items marked **ASSUMED** are strongest-reasonable assumptions pending the logo workstream or a Fritz call — each carries a decision point.

---

## 1. FAMtastic Definition (canonical, verbatim)

> **FAMtastic** (adj.): Fearless deviation from established norms with a bold and unapologetic commitment to stand apart on purpose, applying mastery of craft to the point that the results are the proof, and manifesting the extraordinary from the ordinary.

Rules of use:
- The definition appears **on the site, styled as a dictionary entry** (word, part of speech, definition) — it is product mechanics, not decoration. Default home placement: immediately after the entry scene, before the first proof module.
- Footer micro-line everywhere: *"FAMtastic is not a typo. It's the method."*
- Working phrase (hero/CTA copy source): *"From Proof to Profit: See how the FAMtastic Way can glow up your business."*
- Brand personality stack, in priority order, governs every visual call: **(1) bold/creative** ("Wow, this is who I'm working with?"), **(2) tech-genius/AI-powered**, **(3) corporate/professional**, with **street-smart entrepreneur energy** throughout. When two treatments tie, pick the bolder one.

## 2. FAM Capitalization Logic

- The brand renders **FAMtastic** — F, A, M uppercase, "tastic" lowercase — in **all** contexts: headings, body copy, nav, meta titles, alt text, JSON-LD. Never "Famtastic", "FAMTastic", or "famtastic" (lowercase permitted only in domains/slugs/code identifiers).
- **FAM is the impact point; "tastic" is secondary.** In display contexts FAM gets heavier weight/width/color; "tastic" never out-weighs it.
- "Designs" is the **identifier**, visually subordinate to both: smaller, tracked-out, single neutral color.
- CSS hierarchy contract (used by the text-logo component, §4): `.fam` > `.tastic` > `.designs` in visual weight, always.

## 4. Temporary Text/Logo Treatment

Until the redesign lands, the mark is a **styled text component** — `<BrandMark />` — never an image, **never a placeholder SVG** (standing repo rule: logos are real files or styled text, nothing else).

```
FAM        — display face, weight 900, widest width axis, color --fam-burst
             (or tri-color F/A/M letters in hero-scale only)
tastic     — same face, weight 500, normal width, color --fam-text
Designs    — body face, weight 600, 0.14em tracking, uppercase, 0.5em size of FAM,
             color --fam-text-dim, baseline-aligned after "tastic" with 0.35em gap
```

- Nav variant: single-line, FAM in `--fam-burst` only (no tri-color at small sizes — fails contrast and reads as noise).
- Hero variant: tri-color F/A/M letters allowed at ≥ 64px rendered size; subtle radial glow (`--fam-burst` at 12% alpha, 80px blur) permitted behind the M as the burst echo.
- The component is one source of truth (`components/brand/BrandMark.tsx`), with `variant: nav | hero | footer`. No page re-implements it.
- When the new logo ships, `BrandMark` swaps internals; call sites do not change.

Usage rules:
- `--fam-burst` is the **only** color allowed on primary CTAs. One burst-colored element per viewport-height of content (excluding nav CTA).

- Contrast floor: 4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text/UI — `--fam-burst` on `--fam-ink` passes for large text/buttons; body text never sits in burst green.
- Light-context exception: long-form legal/FAQ pages may use an inverted `--fam-paper #F4F7FA` scheme; everything marketing stays dark stage.

## 7. Spacing / Grid

- **Base unit 4px.** Scale (Tailwind-aligned): `4 · 8 · 12 · 16 · 24 · 32 · 48 · 64 · 96 · 128 · 160`.
- Section vertical rhythm: `--space-section: clamp(96px, 12vh, 160px)`; hero/scene sections are full-viewport (`min-height: 100svh`).
- Grid: **12-column**, max content width **1280px**, gutter 32px desktop / 20px ≤ 768px, outer padding 24px mobile / 48px desktop. Full-bleed allowed only for scenes, dividers, and CTA bands.
- **Z-pattern default** for marketing sections: heading top-left → supporting visual top-right → proof/detail sweep → CTA bottom-right. Campaign pain-mirror sections invert to F-pattern (scannable list left). Every section's terminal focal point is its CTA or the next section's hook — no dead-end corners.
- Card internals: 24px padding (32px for proof modules), 16px stack gap, 12px radius (`--radius-card`), 8px radius controls, 999px pills.

## 8. Immersive Design Principles

The site is a **digital showroom, not a normal site with a 3D hero** (plan §6). Operating principles:

1. **Transformation is the subject.** Every scene/section shows ordinary → extraordinary in motion (the Proof-to-Profit narrative). If a visual doesn't depict or support transformation, cut it.
2. **One immersion spine per page.** A page gets one continuous scroll-driven narrative (GSAP ScrollTrigger timeline); sections are beats on that spine, not isolated widgets.
3. **Tier discipline (launch-blocking sequencing):** Tier-1 (CSS/WebGL motion, parallax, Lottie/Rive, GSAP) ships first and must carry the wow alone. Tier-2 (one R3F entry scene + campaign paths) matures behind it. Tier-3 (generated video) deferred — leave `<slot>` placeholders, build nothing.
4. **Immersive, not cluttered; premium, not gimmicky.** Max 2 simultaneously animating focal elements per viewport. Idle/ambient motion ≤ 1 element (e.g., burst glow breathing).
5. **Every scene ends at a CTA** (hard rule, mirrored in agents.md §1.4 quality checklist). A scroll narrative that resolves anywhere else fails review.
6. **The showroom proves the tracking promise**: dashboard visuals (§16) and proof modules (§15) are the same components later powering real client views — design them as products, not props.
7. **Static first paint is part of the design**, not a loading state: every scene's fallback still (§12) is art-directed and shippable on its own.

## 9. Motion Rules

Library: **GSAP + ScrollTrigger** for scroll narratives; CSS transitions for micro-interactions; Framer Motion permitted for React UI state only (no double-driving one element with two libs).

| Class | Duration | Easing | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Micro | 150–200ms | `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0.38, 0.9)` | Hover, focus, toggles |
| Standard | 320ms | `--ease-fam-out: cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)` | Card enter, accordion, menu |
| Emphatic | 600ms | `--ease-fam-out` | Section headline reveals, proof card flips |
| Burst | 800ms | `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.4, 0.64, 1)` (single mild overshoot) | The signature radial scale-up — key moments only, ≤ 1/section |
| Scene | scrub-bound | ScrollTrigger `scrub: 0.8` | Camera/timeline tied to scroll |

Rules:
- **Scroll-scrubbed beats are the default** for narrative motion (user controls time); autonomous animation only for entrances and ambient glow.
- Entrance reveals: y-translate 24px + fade, stagger 70ms, max 6 staggered children (rest appear instantly).
- ScrollTrigger pins: max one pinned scene per page besides the hero; pin duration ≤ 2.5× viewport heights.
- Never animate `top/left/width/height` — transforms and opacity only. `will-change` applied just-in-time and removed after.
- Count-up numbers (dashboards): 1.2s, ease-out, mono face, only fire once on first intersection.
- No autoplaying motion before LCP element paints (perf rule §11).

## 10. 3D Rules (Tier-2 — R3F + drei)

These are inputs/constraints for every `ScenePlan` (agents.md §1.4). The Planner may compose within them, never beyond them.

- **One `<Canvas>` per page, ever.** The entry scene owns it; campaign paths are beats inside the same scene graph.
- Budgets (per scene, hard): **≤ 600KB gzipped** scene chunk (code + geometry), **≤ 1.5MB** total textures (KTX2/basis compressed), **≤ 150k triangles** in view, **≤ 3 texture sets @ ≤ 1024px**, draw calls ≤ 80 (instancing mandatory for repeated nodes — agent-worker nodes are one instanced mesh).
- Lighting: **baked** (lightmaps/vertex AO) + max 1 realtime light for the burst pass. No realtime shadows.
- `dpr={[1, 1.75]}` clamped; `frameloop="demand"` outside active scroll ranges; pause rendering on `document.hidden` and when canvas exits viewport.
- Camera motion is scroll-scrubbed (ScrollTrigger drives a normalized progress → camera path), never free-flying on its own; drag adds ±parallax only (max 6° orbit) — users never get lost.
- Loading: lazy `dynamic(() => import(...), { ssr: false })`; styled static first paint (§11) renders immediately; canvas cross-fades in (320ms) only after `useProgress` hits 100% **and** LCP has fired.
- Interaction targets in 3D must have DOM mirrors (real `<a>`/`<button>` overlaid) — keyboard/AT never depends on raycasting.
- Mobile (< 768px or `navigator.hardwareConcurrency ≤ 4`): serve the Lite variant (same narrative, ≤ 50% budgets) or the composed-still fallback. The Planner decides which in the ScenePlan (`fallback_still` is mandatory either way).

## 11. Performance Rules (launch-blocking — plan §6/§36)

| Gate | Threshold |
|---|---|
| LCP, mid-range mobile (throttled 4x CPU, Slow 4G) | **< 2.5s** |
| Lighthouse performance, mobile, every public page | **≥ 80** |
| CLS | < 0.1 |
| First-load JS (marketing routes, excl. lazy 3D chunk) | ≤ 180KB gz (ASSUMED budget; tighten if Lighthouse demands) |
| Fonts | 3 families max, variable, subset latin, `font-display: swap`, preloaded |

Mechanics:
- **3D loads progressively behind a styled static first paint**: the LCP element is always a static composed image/text block (`next/image`, AVIF/WebP, priority) — never the canvas.
- All routes SSG/ISR (App Router static export semantics); zero client JS on legal/FAQ pages.
- Images: `next/image` everywhere, AVIF first, explicit dimensions (CLS), lazy below fold.
- Lottie/Rive: ≤ 250KB per animation, loaded on intersection, Rive preferred where interactivity matters.
- CI gate: Lighthouse CI on every PR touching public routes; a score < 80 mobile **blocks merge** (mirrors §36 acceptance rubric).

## 12. Accessibility / Reduced-Motion Rules

- **`prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` swaps every scene for its composed still** — an art-directed static image + the same copy + the same CTA. No scrubbing, no parallax, no count-ups (final numbers render directly), no autoplay. This is a designed deliverable, defined in each ScenePlan (`reduced_motion_behavior`, `fallback_still`) **before** the animated version is built.
- Full keyboard navigability: logical tab order, visible focus (`outline: 2px solid var(--fam-burst); outline-offset: 2px`), skip-to-content link, scenes never trap focus, 3D interactions mirrored in DOM (§10).
- Contrast per §5; UI text never below 14px; touch targets ≥ 44×44px.
- Semantic landmarks per page (`header/nav/main/footer`), one `h1`, sections headed in order.
- Motion never carries sole meaning: any state shown by animation also shown by text/icon.
- Every scene ends at a CTA reachable without JS-driven scroll (CTA band is real DOM after the scene container).

## 14. CTA Rules

- **Primary CTA = "Get a Preview"** → `/preview` (plan §5: primary CTA target). Campaign pages may swap to their claim-path CTA; everything else defaults here.
- Primary style: `--fam-burst` fill, `--fam-ink` text, 999px pill, 16px/28px padding, weight 700; hover = `--fam-burst-deep` border + 1.03 scale (micro timing); never gradient-filled.
- Secondary: 1px `--fam-line` border ghost button, `--fam-text`; used for "See the proof", "Book a call".
- **One primary CTA per viewport** (nav CTA exempt). Two primaries visible at once is a review failure.
- Every page ends in a `CTABand`; every scene's final beat lands on a CTA (hard rules, §8/§12).
- Church/nonprofit/professional surfaces always pair the pay CTA with a **talk-first** secondary ("Book a call") — committee dynamics, plan §11 block 8.
- CTA copy is verb-first, outcome-flavored, ≤ 4 words ("Get Your Preview", "Claim Your Proof", "See It Live"). Never "Submit", "Learn More", "Click Here".

## 15. Proof Card Rules (floating proof modules)

The commercial-style cards showcasing transformations (plan §6 mechanic 5) — the "engine to build more scenes":

- Anatomy: business category eyebrow (mono face) → **before/after visual** (slider or auto cross-fade, 600ms emphatic) → 1-line transformation statement → outcome line → CTA ("See the full story" → proof gallery, or "Get yours" → /preview).
- Visual: `--fam-surface` card, 12px radius, 1px `--fam-line` border, floating treatment = 24px soft shadow + 6px y-float on scroll parallax; hover tilt ≤ 4° (desktop pointer only); reduced-motion = static after-state with a "before" thumbnail inset.
- **Truth rules (non-negotiable, mirrors plan §11/§27):** only real, permissioned proofs; outcome lines use directional language tied to observed facts — **no invented metrics, no fabricated testimonials, no stock "results"**. Pre-launch, cards show factory demo builds labeled "Demo build" honestly.
- Cards are composable as headers/sections anywhere; max 3 per row desktop, 1 per row mobile, stagger-revealed (§9).

## 16. Dashboard Visual Rules

Animated mock dashboards prove the "Grow It" tracking promise (plan §6 mechanic 4) and later power real client dashboards:

- Metrics by campaign context: giving (church), donations/volunteers (nonprofit), calls/bookings (local), consultations/leads (professional).
- Style: `--fam-surface-2` panel, mono face figures, `--fam-burst` spark-lines/positive deltas, 8px-radius tiles in a 2×2 (mobile) / 4×1 (desktop) grid; count-up per §9 motion rules.
- **Honesty rule:** mock dashboards on marketing pages carry a quiet "Illustrative" caption (`--fs-small`, `--fam-text-dim`) — the design must wow without lying (no fabricated client numbers, plan §27 / Analytics rule "numbers from SQL only" extends to marketing).
- Charts are pure SVG/CSS (no chart library on marketing routes — JS budget §11); the real-dashboard versions may add a library later behind auth.

## 18. Forbidden Cookie-Cutter Patterns (review-failing offenses)

Any of these fails Brand/Voice Guardian and Quality Review — concrete, not vibes:

1. **Purple/blue linear "AI gradient"** backgrounds, buttons, or text fills (the generic AI-startup wash). Radial burst light per §5 is the only gradient language.
2. **Stock agency layout:** centered hero + 3 feature cards + logo strip + testimonial carousel + footer. The home page is a scroll narrative, not a stack of standard blocks.
3. **Inter (or system-default) typography without intent** — the type stack is §6; substituting defaults is a regression, not a simplification.
4. Generic copy: "We build websites that convert", "Elevate your brand", "Solutions", "Empower". Voice rule: create the need around *see*; no jargon walls.
5. **Placeholder anything visible:** lorem ipsum, placeholder SVG logos (standing rule), stock "team at whiteboard" photography, gray image boxes.
6. Fake social proof: invented testimonials, fabricated star ratings, made-up client counts, "as seen on" logos without basis (§15/§16 honesty rules).
7. Floating emoji/blob decorations, tilted-screenshot perspective heroes, dot-grid + glow "SaaS hero" composition.
8. Carousel/slider as primary content navigation; auto-rotating testimonials.
9. Templated section symmetry — every section the same centered-heading + 3-column rhythm. Use the z-pattern variance (§7); consecutive sections must not share an identical layout skeleton.
10. Chat-widget popups, exit-intent modals, cookie-banner theatrics beyond legal minimum, fake "23 people are viewing this" urgency.
11. A 3D hero bolted onto an otherwise static page — the immersion spine (§8.2) is page-wide or the 3D doesn't ship.
12. Burst green on non-CTA elements at body scale (dilutes the signal, §5).

If deadline pressure forces simplification, simplify by **cutting scope** (fewer sections, Tier-1 only), never by reverting to these patterns — cookie-cutter drift is the named anti-thesis risk (plan §33).
