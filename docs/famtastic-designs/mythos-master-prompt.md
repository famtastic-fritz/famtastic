## Core Brand Definition

FAMtastic is not a typo. It is the method.

**FAMtastic** *(adj.)*: Fearless deviation from established norms with a bold and unapologetic commitment to stand apart on purpose, applying mastery of craft to the point that the results are the proof, and manifesting the extraordinary from the ordinary.

For FAMtastic Designs, the business is not merely selling websites. It is selling transformation:

- Vision: show what a business, church, nonprofit, professional, or brand could become.
- Proof: create a real preview, not just a promise.
- Presence: make clients look bigger, sharper, trusted, and professional online.
- Profit: connect design to calls, bookings, sales, donations, leads, payments, and engagement.
- Systems: use AI, automation, mobile tools, and workflows to help clients operate smarter.
- Transformation: manifest the extraordinary from the ordinary.

Working phrase:

> From Proof to Profit: See how the FAMtastic Way can glow up your business.

## Multi-Agent Swarm Requirement

The plan must define a full orchestrated, trackable agent swarm.

Do not design a single-agent system.

Do not make Mythos do all the worker tasks.

Design a system where high-value planning uses stronger models and repetitive tasks use cheaper capable models/tools.

For each agent, define:

- Agent name.
- Purpose.
- Inputs.
- Outputs.
- Best model/tool.
- Cheaper fallback model/tool.
- Quality-check method.
- Cost risk.
- Failure modes.
- Tracking metrics.
- Human review trigger.
- Lessons-learned logging.
- Skill/workflow created after success.

Include agents for at least:

- Strategy architect.
- Brand/voice guardian.
- UX/site architect.
- Immersive/3D design planner.
- Lead discovery.
- Lead scoring.
- Presence scanner.
- Campaign strategist.
- Proof generator.
- Outreach writer.
- Email compliance/deliverability checker.
- Quality review.
- Pricing/package recommender.
- Payment/onboarding router.
- Fulfillment task planner.
- Analytics/results tracker.
- Workstream manager.
- Lessons-learned/skills librarian.
- Cost auditor.
- Repository/doc writer.

Model/tool routing principle:

- Premium models only for architecture, strategy, hard reasoning, and final quality review.
- Mid-tier models for copy, plans, synthesis, and structured docs.
- Cheap/fast models for tagging, classification, parsing, cleanup, summaries, and repetitive operations.
- Coding-specialized models/tools for implementation.
- Research-specialized models/tools when general research is weak.
- Avoid local models for now unless proven, tested, and protected by quality checks.

The system must make it possible to say:

- Which agent did this?
- Which model/tool did it use?
- What did it cost?
- What did it miss?
- What failed?
- What improved after changing the model/tool?
- What reusable skill/workflow was created?

## Automation Philosophy

The MVP should be automation-first with human exception handling.

Fritz has multiple obligations and cannot manually operate the funnel every day.

Agents should do repeated work:

- Lead discovery.
- Lead scoring.
- Classification.
- Presence scanning.
- Proof generation.
- Outreach draft generation.
- AI quality review.
- Email sending after pass/fail gate.
- Click tracking.
- Deposit/payment routing.
- Onboarding form routing.
- Fulfillment task creation.
- Workstream tracking.
- Lessons learned.

Fritz should review exceptions:

- High-value leads.
- Low quality scores.
- Sensitive church/nonprofit language.
- Payment/claim issues.
- Custom prospect replies.
- Automation uncertainty.

Start with AI quality review plus Fritz only for flagged cases. Move toward auto-send only after the system proves itself.
