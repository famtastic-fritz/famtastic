What this mockup shows

1Tool rail (Cowork pattern)
Left rail is pluggable. Tools register; rail accommodates. "Add tool" at the bottom is the discovery seam. Active tools (Workbench + Shay chat) are gold; inspectors and editors sit dim until the task needs them. Phase-3 tools (Terminal) appear with "soon" labels so the roadmap is visible without being noise.
2Handoff context inherited
The "Handoff from Shay-Shay" pill at the top + the inherited payload card on the bench prove the contract works. Workshop opens with full context — source page, original artifact, intent, prior attempts, success criteria, follow-up jobs. Nothing has to be re-explained.
3The bench has the artifact
Original image on the left, three attempts moving right, the winner marked. This is the "thing on the workbench" — the user can see exactly what's being worked on, not abstracted into chat messages.
4Shay docks near the chat
The chat panel on the right is Shay-in-the-Workshop. Same Shay as Shay-Shay — different state (SHAY_THINKING). Her orb pulses while she works. She summarizes her actions in chat and offers next steps as chips, not paragraphs.
5Job queue is real, not decorative
All jobs route through lib/job-queue.js (the existing system, per v2 doc). Real durations, real costs. Queued items show approval gates ("save_to_assets · queued · waits for approve") — nothing destructive runs without your nod.
6Return path is visible
Status bar bottom right shows where this session ends — "→ Shay-Shay (Media Studio · image_grid)". When you approve, Shay-Shay on the original page hears about it and updates what she shows you next. The loop closes.
