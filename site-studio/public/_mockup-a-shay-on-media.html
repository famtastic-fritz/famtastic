What this mockup shows

1Page-context strip
The gold strip near the top of Shay's panel proves she sees the page. Not "I'm a chatbot, ask me anything" — "I see 6 images, img_002 is selected." That string is generated from the page's registered context provider, not from the user's question.
2Inline visual reasoning
When she answers "which is warmest", she returns a thumbnail of her pick + ranked reasoning. She's not just text — she shows the actual image she's recommending because she can see it.
3Action chips, not advice
"Select img_003" / "Refine prompt for warmer" are real buttons. Click and the action runs on the page. That's the multifunctional utility — she does, she doesn't just describe.
4"Take to Workshop" CTA
The gold chip with the arrow is the handoff trigger. Click and the structured payload (page, artifact, intent, recent actions, success criteria) travels with her to the Workshop. The Workshop opens with full context — no re-asking.
5Status bar shows the wiring
"Shay-Shay context provider: media_studio.image_grid ✓" — explicit confirmation that this page registered with Shay's context registry. When this is missing on a page, you'll see a red x. That's how we know the wiring landed.
6One panel, one composer
No floating orb fighting with this panel. No second composer somewhere else. Shay lives in one consistent place per page. The orb identity (animated, personality) is a future visual phase — this is the structural baseline.
