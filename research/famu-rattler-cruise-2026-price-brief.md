## 11) How to get around hidden forms, session state, and dynamic pricing

Short answer:
- Stop treating cruise sites like static catalogs.
- Treat each quote as a stateful product.
- Track scenarios, not just pages.

### Verified source anchors
- Royal Caribbean group travel page confirms a formal group-planning lane and phone support at (800) 465-3595:
  - https://www.royalcaribbean.com/plan-a-cruise/group-travel
- Royal Caribbean FAQ states posted rates are based on double occupancy:
  - https://www.royalcaribbean.com/aus/en/faq/questions/can-i-purchase-a-stateroom-for-one-person
- Royal Caribbean FAQ states published rates may increase or decrease without prior notice:
  - https://www.royalcaribbean.com/faq/questions/price-change-information

### Friction taxonomy
1. Occupancy-gated pricing
   - A cabin is not one price. It becomes a different quote product at 1, 2, 3, or 4 guests.
2. Session/form-gated inventory
   - Some fare classes and cabin options only appear after traveler count, guest mix, residency, promo qualifiers, or booking path are entered.
3. Total-price opacity
   - Taxes, fees, port expenses, gratuities, promo structure, and event add-ons can change the real payable total materially.
4. Channel mismatch
   - OTA pages, direct booking flows, and group/travel-advisor lanes do not reliably expose the same inventory.
5. Dynamic pricing
   - Rates can move intraday or between booking paths.

### The right workaround
Build a quote matrix instead of scraping a single listing page.

Canonical quote dimensions:
- ship
- sailing date
- cabin category
- occupancy
- guest mix
- residency / market / currency
- refundable vs nonrefundable
- group code or advisor/group lane
- event add-on requirement

Each combination above is a separate monitored scenario.

### Practical technical pattern
1. Use public pages for initial discovery only.
2. Use Playwright for stateful quote capture once occupancy and guest details matter.
3. Persist session state after a valid quote flow is reached.
4. Revisit stable quote/deep-link states instead of replaying the full search funnel every time.
5. Store normalized totals, not just teaser per-person prices.
6. Compare direct lane, OTA lane, and group/advisor lane for the same normalized scenario.

### What to automate
- repeated quote retrieval for fixed scenarios
- screenshot capture and raw snapshot storage
- public-page monitoring for visible deltas
- channel comparison
- threshold alerts on total-price drops or new cabin availability
- advisor/group quote logging once numbers come back by email or phone

### What NOT to automate
- payment or booking submission
- fake identities, fake residency, or promo abuse
- aggressive high-frequency scraping
- private API abuse or access-control bypass
- brittle "8 clicks from homepage" flows when saved quote/session state exists

### Recommended v1 stack
- Playwright for browser-state capture
- Python + FastAPI for orchestration/API layer
- Postgres for quote history
- APScheduler or cron for low-frequency checks
- changedetection.io for simple page-level watch tasks
- optional later: Crawlee / Apify for session-pool and retry hardening

### Captain conclusion
The clean way around cruise-pricing friction is not a hack. It is a structured quote-monitoring system that understands occupancy, state, and channel differences.

## 12) How FAMtastic benefits if we start our own travel agency

Short answer:
- The friction is the moat.
- Expedia-style players win on generic search.
- A FAMtastic-style agency wins on messy, high-touch, hard-to-compare travel.

### Why the friction helps us
1. It creates advisor relevance
   - Customers cannot easily compare apples to apples.
2. It rewards repricing vigilance
   - Monitoring fare changes over time becomes a real service.
3. It increases the value of group orchestration
   - Reunions, alumni trips, creator trips, and hosted events have coordination pain that generic OTAs do not solve well.
4. It protects margin
   - When value is hidden in booking path, perks, policy timing, and group handling, service fees make more sense.
5. It creates proprietary operating data
   - A long-running quote history becomes a real asset.

### Best FAMtastic wedge
Do NOT launch as a generic travel agency.

Launch as:
- group / affinity / event travel concierge
- with automated fare watch + repricing support
- and human handling for the messy parts

Best-fit niches:
- alumni cruises
- reunions
- creator/community hosted sailings
- milestone trips
- church / club / affinity group departures

### Money lanes
1. Supplier commissions
2. Group-booking margin and organizer-value optimization
3. Planning / concierge fees
4. Repricing-monitoring subscription or success fee
5. Ancillaries:
   - insurance
   - hotel
   - transfers
   - excursions
   - dining / celebration upgrades
6. Membership / "travel desk" support for repeat clients or communities

FAMtastic Fare Watch Concierge

Why this is strong:

### Captain conclusion
The winning business is not "we found a cheap cruise."

## 15) Generalized lessons to prevent drift

These lessons are intentionally generalized so they remain reusable beyond this cruise task.

1. Separate the immediate user ask from strategic expansion.
   - First answer the narrow decision question.
   - Then deepen into systems, business opportunity, or architecture as a later wave.

2. Preserve a baseline answer before expanding scope.
   - Once a decision-grade answer exists, do not let later analysis blur or overwrite it.
   - Deeper waves should refine, challenge, or extend the answer — not replace it silently.

3. Reopen the canonical artifact before summarizing after interruption, compression, or redirection.
   - If context gets noisy, compressed, or contested, ground back into the saved file first.
   - Artifact-first recovery is safer than memory-first recovery.

4. Separate verified public numbers from likely better manual/hidden paths.
   - A self-serve public quote and a likely cheaper manual-quote path are not the same thing.
   - Label both clearly.

5. Normalize to total outcome, not teaser pricing.
   - Compare like-for-like scenarios.
   - Add required add-ons, access requirements, and scenario-specific costs before making recommendations.

6. Keep observation and interpretation separate.
   - Observation = what was directly verified.
   - Interpretation = what the evidence most likely means.

7. Keep strategic spin-offs in a dedicated parking lane.
   - If a task reveals a product, service, or revenue opportunity, capture it in a labeled section instead of letting it swallow the main ask.

8. Assessment-grade work needs inspectable method, not just a strong conclusion.
   - Preserve lanes, sources, confidence levels, and artifact paths while the work is happening.

### B. Florida seller-of-travel compliance baseline

Official FDACS rule shape:
- Any seller of travel with a business location in Florida, or that offers to sell travel-related services in Florida for individuals or groups, must register annually unless exempt.
- Sellers of travel are required to provide proof of assurance in the form of a performance bond, not to exceed `$25,000`.
- If selling vacation certificates, the assurance amount can be up to `$50,000`.
- Source: FDACS Sellers of Travel page and FDACS `Who is required to register?` page.

Independent sales agent path:
- FDACS form `FDACS-10211` is the Sellers of Travel Independent Sales Agents Application.
- It requires a `$50` annual filing fee per independent sales agent.
- The independent agent must:
  1. act for or on behalf of a seller of travel operating in compliance with Florida Sellers of Travel law,
  2. have a written contract with the seller(s) of travel and provide a copy,
  3. not receive a fee, commission, or other valuable consideration directly from purchasers of travel or travel-related services,
  4. not possess un-issued ticket stock,
  5. not have the ability to issue tickets, lodging/vacation certificates, or other travel documents.
- Source: `https://forms.fdacs.gov/10211.pdf`

ARC exemption note:
- FDACS states persons contracted with ARC for three years or more under the same ownership/control are not required to register, but must have a statement of exemption from FDACS to obtain an occupational license.
- Sellers of travel offering vacation certificates need five years or more with ARC under same ownership/control for that exemption path.
